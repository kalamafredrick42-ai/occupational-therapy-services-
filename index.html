About

A clinician focused on function, not just diagnosis

Fredrick Kalama is an occupational therapist based in Nairobi, working with children and adults to close the gap between a diagnosis and daily life — whether that's a child learning to hold a pencil, an adult regaining hand strength after surgery, or a family navigating therapy together.

  • Focus areas Pediatric development, physical dysfunction rehabilitation, family & mental health support
  • Approach Assessment-led, individualised intervention plans reviewed at every stage
  • Setting In-clinic consultation and therapy sessions, Nairobi

Services

Three areas of practice, one aim: restored independence

Every plan starts with a consultation to understand the person, not just the condition.

01 — Pediatric

Pediatric Occupational Therapy

Supporting children's developmental, sensory, and motor milestones so they can participate fully at home and school.

  • Fine and gross motor skill development
  • Sensory processing support
  • School-readiness and handwriting
  • Play-based intervention
02 — Rehabilitation

Physical Dysfunction Therapy

Rebuilding strength, coordination, and independence after injury, surgery, or a physical condition affecting daily function.

  • Post-injury and post-surgical rehab
  • Strength and coordination retraining
  • Adaptive strategies for daily tasks
  • Progress tracked against function goals
03 — Mental & Family

Mental & Family Therapy

Working with individuals and families together, since recovery and daily functioning are rarely one person's task alone.

  • Family therapy sessions
  • Coping and routine-building strategies
  • Caregiver guidance and support
  • Ongoing, collaborative sessions

How it works

From first consultation to ongoing therapy

The same four-step path applies across pediatric, physical, and family therapy.

01

Consultation

An initial assessment to understand the person's history, goals, and current function.

02

Intervention plan

A therapy plan built around specific, realistic function goals.

03

Therapy sessions

Regular hands-on sessions — pediatric, physical, or family-based.

04

Review

Progress reviewed and the plan adjusted as function improves.
